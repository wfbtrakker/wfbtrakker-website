# LinkTray - Your Personal Link Organization System

## Never Lose Another Important Link Again

**LinkTray** is a lightweight Windows system tray application that puts all your important links, bookmarks, and file paths exactly where you need them - right at your fingertips. Say goodbye to cluttered browser bookmarks and endless searching through folders. LinkTray organizes everything in a clean, hierarchical menu that's always just one click away.

## Key Features

### Instant Access from Your System Tray

Your links live in your system tray, always available without opening your browser or interrupting your workflow. One click on the tray icon shows your entire link collection, organized exactly the way you want it.

**Benefits:**

### One-Click Browser URL Capture

Capture links from your browser faster than you ever thought possible. With a simple hotkey (default: CTRL+L), LinkTray instantly grabs the URL from your active browser window and adds it to your collection. No copying, no pasting, no dialog boxes to fill out.

**How It Works:**
1. Browse to any web page
2. Press CTRL+L (or your custom hotkey)
3. LinkTray automatically captures the URL and domain name
4. Your link is instantly added to your collection
5. Continue working without interruption

**Supports:**
- Google Chrome
- Microsoft Edge
- Mozilla Firefox
- All modern web browsers

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Can I sync my links across multiple computers?
Currently, LinkTray stores all data locally. You can manually export and import your link collection by copying the data file.

### Does LinkTray work with all browsers?
Yes! The hotkey capture feature works with Chrome, Edge, Firefox, and all modern browsers. Opening links uses your system's default browser.

### How many links can I store?
There's no hard limit. LinkTray can handle thousands of links without performance degradation.

### Can I import my browser bookmarks?
Currently, bookmarks need to be added manually. However, the CTRL+L hotkey makes it very quick to capture existing bookmarks by visiting them and pressing the hotkey.

### Is my data backed up?
LinkTray saves your data locally in a single file. You can manually back up this file to ensure you never lose your links.

### Can I customize the icons?
The current version uses standard folder and link icons. Icon customization is planned for a future release.

### What happens if I delete the Favorites folder?
The Favorites folder is protected and cannot be deleted. It's automatically managed by LinkTray based on your usage patterns.

### Can I disable click count tracking?
Yes! Click counts are optional and can be toggled on/off in the Settings tab. When disabled, no tracking occurs.

### How do I change the hotkey?
Go to the Settings tab in the Link Manager and configure your preferred modifier keys and base key. Changes apply immediately.

### Can I organize links across multiple monitors?
Yes! LinkTray works seamlessly with multi-monitor setups. The tray icon appears on your primary taskbar, and links open on the appropriate monitor based on your Windows settings.

## Tips and Tricks

### Power User Shortcuts
- **Right-click nodes** in the tree for quick Add Link/Folder options
- **Press Enter** in dialogs to quickly add links
- **Drag to empty space** below the tree to move items to the root level
- **Double-click URLs** at the bottom of the window to open them
- **Use Up/Down buttons** to quickly reorder links without dragging

### Organization Best Practices
- **Use descriptive names** that tell you what the link is at a glance
- **Create shallow hierarchies** (2-3 levels deep maximum) for faster navigation
- **Group by task or project**, not by link type
- **Use the Favorites folder** as your dashboard for current work
- **Review click counts** monthly to prune unused links

### Workflow Integration
- **Capture first, organize later**: Use CTRL+L liberally while working, organize during breaks
- **Create group templates**: Set up a standard structure and copy it for new projects
- **Use consistent naming**: Develop a naming convention across groups
- **Leverage link groups**: Switch groups when context-switching between projects
- **Start with Windows**: Enable auto-start for seamless integration

### Making the Most of Link Groups
- **Client Work**: One group per active client

- **Role-Based**: Separate personal, work, and side project links
- **Learning**: Create a group for current courses or skills you're learning
